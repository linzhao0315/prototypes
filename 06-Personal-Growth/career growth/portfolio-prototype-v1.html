Chapter 01 · The Brief

Before the pipeline,
the brief.

Speed without understanding is just fast failure. Every decision in this project traces back to a single research insight. This is where the story starts.

The Strategic Context
Kiva's Amplify pillar needed new SME + HNWI customers — not enterprise. CSR programs were being defunded. The product flywheel needed a new, self-serve entry point.
My Research Role
I wasn't the researcher. I was the question designer — I defined which product hypotheses needed validation, then worked with the research team to test them against real SME interviews.
The Product Decision
Impact Page + "Try Before You Buy." Not enterprise self-serve tools. The one feature that converts "curious but not committed" into a first deposit — designed for trust, not features.
FINDING 01
SMEs prefer giving locally. Kiva's global reach is a barrier to B2B adoption — not a selling point. Local community relevance drives their giving decisions.
FINDING 02
They frame Kiva as a brand development tool, not a customer acquisition channel. The right metrics are NPS and brand awareness — not conversion rates.
FINDING 03
Personal mission alignment is the green shoot. Most SMEs would consider Kiva for personal giving. B2B starts with making Kiva feel personal — then the business case follows.
The insight that changed the roadmap
"They're not disinterested. They're stuck."
SMEs are willing but stuck — blocked by local relevance gaps, not a lack of mission alignment. This reframed the product question: don't sell the value prop, remove the friction. Design for the aha moment first.
"I would view this as a brand development tool, more so than a customer acquisition or conversion tool… if I wanted to measure the impact of this program, I would want to know: how many people are familiar with and know my brand, and what is their NPS."
SME interview participant · Kiva Partnerships Research · 2026
The research defined what to build. The next question: how fast could we build it without losing the intent?
Chapter 02 · The Build
Kiva Impact Dashboard 2026

From Figma to Live
in 4 Days

The research said: design for trust, not features. But trust is invisible in a Figma file — you only know if you got it right when someone real clicks through it.

The Old Experience
SMEs landing on the Giving Fund page faced a quiet fog. Not hostility — just confusion. Who's raising money? Who is Kiva? Who are the borrowers? Three rounds of user testing kept surfacing the same issue: participants couldn't hold the three entities in their heads at once. They left curious, not committed. Not because they didn't care — because the page gave them nothing solid to hold onto.
The Design Response
Trust signals, not feature lists. Real borrower profiles — "I am independent, and if I am independent, I can do anything I want." Concrete proof: 1 person funded every 90 seconds. 90% quality-of-life improvement, cited from 60 Decibels research. And one clear hierarchy: you raise. Kiva deploys. The design wasn't about aesthetics. It was about removing every reason to hesitate.
The Gap
But static mockups couldn't prove it worked. Every round surfaced comprehension issues. Stakeholders were making decisions from screenshots — guessing at how it would feel. Users were still confused in testing. The design might be right. Might isn't enough to ship. Both groups needed something real to react to.
The Pipeline
🎨 Figma → 🔌 MCP → 🤖 Claude → 🌐 HTML → ✦ Figma
MCP reads exact Figma values. Claude generates the code. Browser validates. Back to Figma for alignment. Not optimizing for speed — optimizing for reality. Something users could click. Something stakeholders could feel.
The Result
Round 3 of user testing, on the live URL: participants finally held it all together — "Lin is raising, Kiva deploys the funds." The confusion cleared. "Try before you buy" moved to high-confidence priority. Eight features went to the backlog. The conversation shifted.

Chapter 05 · The Exploration

Exploring the Boundaries

Speed recovers time. The real question is what you do with it. Two prototypes, 48 hours, two completely different design hypotheses — exploring what's possible when exploration is no longer the bottleneck.

Donation Impact Preview

Focus: Storytelling through data. Skeleton loading states that feel like discovering real people and their impact.

Techniques: D3.js globe with rotating regions, emoji-based borrower markers, Bézier-smoothed counter animations, shimmer overlays, staggered content reveals.

Design question: How do we make data feel human?

Analytics Dashboard Prototype

Focus: Real-time precision. Stat cards that update instantly, bar charts that reveal data in layers, glassmorphic UI with subtle depth.

Techniques: GSAP timelines for staggered animations, gradient bar fills, counter animations with momentum easing, pulsing "LIVE" indicator, responsive layout.

Design question: How do we make speed feel accurate and trustworthy?

View Live →

What I learned: AI doesn't just make you faster at execution. It lets you explore the space of what's possible. In 48 hours, I could prototype 2–3 different animation strategies, test them with real data, and refine based on what actually feels right — not what I thought would feel right.

The old workflow: design → build → iterate (weeks). The new one: ideate → prototype → iterate (days). The quality bar is the same. The exploration depth is 10x deeper.
